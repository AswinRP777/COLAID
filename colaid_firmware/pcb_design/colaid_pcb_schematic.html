üìù Design Notes

  • LM2596 Output: MUST be adjusted to 3.3V with the onboard potentiometer. Measure with a multimeter BEFORE connecting the ESP32!
  • 200¬µF Capacitor: Electrolytic, placed across LM2596 output. Negative (striped) lead ‚Üí GND. Use ‚â•16V rated.
  • ON/OFF Switch: SPST in series with battery positive, BEFORE the LM2596 input.
  • TCS3200 OE Pin: Active LOW ‚Äî tie directly to GND to keep sensor always enabled.
  • TCS3200 Voltage: Operates at 2.7V‚Äì5.5V, so 3.3V is correct. All signals are 3.3V logic compatible.
  • GPIO 2: Built-in blue LED on ESP32 DevKit ‚Äî no external wiring needed.
  • Buzzer: Firmware uses ledcWriteTone() PWM. Passive piezo buzzer preferred for variable tones.
  • Power Budget: ESP32 ~240mA, TCS3200 ~2mA, Buzzer ~30mA ‚Üí ~6-7 hours on 2000 mAh cell.
  • Do NOT connect the battery directly to ESP32 VIN/5V. Always use the LM2596 step-down.

Wiring & Assembly Tips

  • 3.3V & GND Rails: Run continuous solder bridges across 2 full rows near the top of the board. All power connections tap into these rails.
  • ESP32 Mounting: Solder 2 √ó 19-pin female headers. This lets you remove the ESP32 for reprogramming or replacement.
  • TCS3200 Header: Use an 8-pin female header (or 2 √ó 4) on the PCB edge. Connect to the sensor via a ribbon cable / Dupont wires ‚Äî the sensor sits on the eyewear frame, not on the PCB.
  • Battery Connector: Use a 2-pin JST-PH connector so the battery (in a separate holder on the frame arm) can be easily disconnected.
  • Wire Routing: Route signal wires on the solder side (bottom) using enameled copper wire or bare wire between pads. Keep the component side clean.
  • Keep LM2596 away from ESP32: The switching regulator generates EMI. Place it at the far end of the board with the capacitor acting as a buffer.
  • Total Board Weight: Approximately 15‚Äì20g (bare PCB + components, excluding battery and sensor).
